> **Learning note — Retrospective**
> - **Why**: Structured reflection on *how* the team worked — not what was built, but how the team worked together — to improve the next cycle
> - **Who uses it**: PM identifies process improvements that protect future timelines; Engineers surface tooling problems that compound; the team builds a habit of continuous improvement
> - **Key decisions**: What one process change will the team test next cycle? What carried-over items reveal a systemic planning problem?
> - **Next step**: One process change adopted → verified at the next retro; carry-over items dispositioned into the next cycle plan

## What Didn't Work

> **Note — What Didn't Work**: Core of the retrospective — surfaces friction, blockers, and decision failures. Key discipline: specificity. ## Action Items

> **Note — Action Items**: What makes retrospectives valuable rather than just therapeutic — converts identified problems into specific commitments. Key discipline: one concrete, completable action per person per cycle is the sustainable rate — more than that and nothing gets done. Review prior retro action items at the start of each new retro.

## One Process Change

> **Note — One Process Change**: The discipline that prevents retrospectives from producing 15 action items that paralyze the team. Key principle: one small, testable process change per cycle. The "how we'll know it worked" field is critical — a process change that can't be evaluated isn't a hypothesis, it's a preference.

*One thing we'll do differently next cycle. Keep it small and testable.*
