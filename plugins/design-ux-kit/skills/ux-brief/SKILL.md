Create a UX brief for: $ARGUMENTS

Use the template in [ux-brief-template.md](ux-brief-template.md) as the structure.

Follow this process:
1. **Define the design scope** — what experience or flow is being designed? What is explicitly out of scope?
2. **Identify target users** — who are the primary and secondary users? What do we know about their goals, context, and pain points?
3. **Articulate design goals** — what should the user be able to do or feel after this experience? Express as user outcomes, not feature outputs.
4. **List constraints** — platform, technical, accessibility, brand, timeline, or resource constraints that will shape design decisions.
5. **Define success criteria** — how will we know the design is working? Link to measurable signals (task completion rate, error rate, satisfaction score, etc.).
6. **Surface assumptions** — list the top 3–5 assumptions the design is making about users or context that carry the most risk if wrong.
7. **Identify research gaps** — flag what we don't know that could materially affect design direction. Recommend whether user research is needed before proceeding.

Ask clarifying questions if the scope is unclear. Output a brief ready to share with design, product, and engineering stakeholders.
